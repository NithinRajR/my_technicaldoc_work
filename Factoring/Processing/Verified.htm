Verified

After the system successfully verifies the invoices, or when the invoices pass all validations and do not require further verification, they are displayed on the Verified page.

From the Verified page, users can fund a batch of invoices, remove a batch, open or cancel a batch, edit invoices, mark invoices as ineligible, review an existing batch, or add invoices to a pre-existing batch that has not yet been processed.

  • Base Rate %Base Rate %

    The Base Rate % field appears in the Fund Batch pop-up only when the selected client uses a Per Day Rate fee setup.

    • This is a mandatory field.
    • Users must manually enter the base rate percentage in the Fund Batch pop-up.
    • The system uses the entered Base Rate %, along with the Divisor defined in the Factoring Fee Setup page, to calculate the Total Fee %.
    • The system then calculates the Earned Upfront Fee based on the computed Total Fee %.

    Calculation Logic

    The system calculates the Base Rate value using the following formula:

            Base Rate Value = (Rate % / Divisor) × (Fund Date − Due Date) × Gross Invoice Amount

    System Behavior

    When the user enters a Base Rate % and clicks Calculate:

    • The system retrieves the Spread % and Divisor from the Factoring Fee Setup.

    • The system calculates the Spread %.

    • The system calculates the Funding Rate % using the entered Base Rate % and the Divisor.

    • The system updates the Total Fee % by adding the Base Rate % and the Spread %.

    Validation Rules

    • The field accepts numeric values only.
    • Both whole numbers and decimal values are allowed.
    • Negative values are not allowed.
    • The maximum allowed value is 100.
    • Users may enter values with more than three digits and click Calculate, but the system restricts funding and printing the batch in such cases.
    • If the user enters a value greater than 100:

      • Clicking Calculate and then Fund Batch displays the message:
        “Fee rate must be less than or equal to 100.0.”

      • Clicking Calculate and then Print Batch displays the same validation message.

    This ensures that only valid fee rates are used when funding or printing a batch.

  • Clients' available creditClients' available credit

    The system calculates Client’s Available Credit as the difference between the client’s Credit Line (from the Factoring Basic Client Detail page) and the total AR Balance (from the Account Debtor page). This field is read-only.

    Calculation logic
    Client’s Available Credit = Credit Line − Total AR Balance

    Example

    • Credit Line: 0.00
    • Total AR Balance: 1000.00

    Client’s Available Credit = 0.00 − 1000.00 = −1000.00

  • Earned Upfront FeeEarned Upfront Fee

    The Earned Upfront field displays the upfront fee charged to the client when a funding batch is processed. The calculation method depends on the client’s fee setup type.

    • Per Day Rate Fee Setup

    For clients using a Per Day Rate fee setup, the system calculates the Earned Upfront Fee as the sum of the Spread Fee and the Base Rate Fee.

    • Calculation Formula

    Earned Upfront Fee = Spread Fee Amount + Base Rate Amount

    • Rate Calculations

    Spread Fee Per Day = Spread % ÷ 360

    Fee Rate Per Day = Fee Rate % ÷ 360

    Parameter Value
    Invoice Amount 1,000
    Invoice Date 11/04/2025
    Due Date 12/04/2025
    Current Date 12/08/2025
    Days Past Due 4
    Spread % 2%
    Fee Rate % 5%

     

    • Computed Values

    Spread Fee Per Day = 2 ÷ 36000 = 0.000055556

    Fee Rate Per Day = 5 ÷ 36000 = 0.000138889

    Spread Fee Value
    = 0.000055556 × 1000 × 4
    = 0.22222222

    Funding Fee Value
    = Fee Rate Per Day × Invoice Amount × Days Past Due
    = 0.000138889 × 1000 × 4
    = 0.55555556

    Earned Upfront Fee
    = 0.22222222 + 0.55555556
    = 0.77777778

     

    • Tier-Based Fee Setup

    For clients using a Tier-Based fee setup, the Earned Upfront field displays the upfront fee calculated based on predefined fee tiers.

    This field is read-only

    The system calculates the value automatically when a funding batch is selected

    Fee tiers are defined in the Factoring Fee Setup page

     

    • Fee Tier Structure

    Each tier defines:

    A range of fee days

    A corresponding Total Fee % 

    This behavior depends on the configuration in Factoring Basic Parameters.

     

  • Understanding Fee TiersUnderstanding Fee Tiers

    Fee Tiers are used to calculate Earned Upfront Fees and Accrued Fees on factored invoices. Each tier defines a range of days and an associated Total Fee Percentage, which determines the total fee charged to the client based on the age of an invoice.

    Fee calculations are driven by the Fee Days value of an invoice. Fee Days are calculated either from the Invoice Date or the Funding Date, depending on the configuration selected on the Factoring Basic Parameters page.

    • Fee Tier Structure

    Each Fee Tier consists of:

    Days From – Start of the fee day range

    Days To – End of the fee day range

    Total Fee % – Total fee applicable for invoices that fall within the defined day range

    Note: The Total Fee % is determined by the invoice’s position within the configured Fee Tiers based on Fee Days.

    The Total Fee is determined based on the invoice’s Fee Days as of the current date and the applicable Fee Tier.

    • Formula:

    Total Fees = Earned Upfront Fee + Accrued Fee

    Earned Upfront Fee = (Invoice Amount or Advance Amount) × Total Fee %

    Accrued Fee = Total Fee − Earned Upfront Fee

    • Accrued Fee Calculation Logic:

    Total fees charged on an invoice consist of two components:

    Earned Upfront Fee

    Accrued Fee

    Accrued Fee = Total Fee − Earned Upfront Fee

    Accrued Fee = 10% − 5% = 5%

    The 5% fee charged at funding is the Earned Upfront Fee.

    • Total Fee

    The Total Fee represents the full fee charged to the client for the invoice from the Invoice Date or Funding Date through the Payment Date.

    Example

    If the Fee Days for an invoice at the time of payment equal 7 days, the invoice falls within Tier 2 (6–10 days). If Tier 2 carries a 10% Total Fee, then the Total Fee charged is 10%.

    • Accrued Fee

    The Accrued Fee is the portion of the Total Fee that has been earned but not yet charged to the client.

    Example

    Continuing the earlier example:

    Earned Upfront Fee = 5%

    Fee Days as of 12/10/2025 = 8 days

    Invoice now falls under Tier 2 (6–10 days)

    Total Fee = 10%

    This behavior is controlled by the selection made in the Factoring Basic Parameters configuration.

    The calculated Fee Days determine which Fee Tier applies and, in turn, which Total Fee % is used.

    • Earned Upfront Fee

    The Earned Upfront Fee is a one-time fee charged by the Factor at the time of funding or purchase of the invoice.

    Example

    Invoice No Invoice Amount Invoice Date Funding Date Fee Calculated On
    Tier1 $10,000.00 12/05/2025 12/08/2025 Invoice Amount

    In this example:

    Fee Days = 3 days (calculated from Invoice Date to Funding Date)

    The invoice falls within Tier 1 (1–5 days)

    Total Fee % for Tier 1 = 5%

    Multiple Fee Tiers may be configured to support tiered pricing based on invoice aging.

    • Fee Days Determination

    Fee Days represent the number of days an invoice has been outstanding. The system calculates Fee Days based on:

    • Invoice Date
    • Funding Date
