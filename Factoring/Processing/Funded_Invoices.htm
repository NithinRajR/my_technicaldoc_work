Funded Invoices

The Funded Invoices page is used to track invoices that have been funded and are currently open (Invoice Balance > 0).

Invoices that are fully paid or have an invoice balance of zero are not displayed in this grid. Once the full payment is processed for an invoice, it is automatically removed from the Funded Invoices grid.

From this page, users can:

  • Reverse a funded batch, provided that no payments have been processed against any invoice in the batch.
  • Process chargebacks for invoices that have already been funded.

    The Direct Upload feature allows CYNC users to upload invoices along with Earned Upfront fees directly into the application, bypassing CYNC’s automatic fee calculation process.

    This option is available to authorized users on the Funded Invoices page, where they can upload a file containing invoice and fee details.

    After the upload is completed successfully:

    • The uploaded fee data is displayed in CYNC as Earned Upfront fees.
    • The uploaded invoice records appear in the Funded Invoices grid.

    Note:

    1. The Advance Rate is always set to 100% for invoices uploaded using the Direct Upload feature.
    2. The Settlement Date field in the upload file is optional.
    3. The following file formats are supported:
      1. XLS
      2. XLSX
      3. CSV
    4. When you reverse a Direct Upload batch after funding, the system permanently deletes all associated records, and you can no longer view those details in the CYNC application.

    The  button is available on the Funded Invoices page to allow Factoring users to reverse a funded batch.

    Reversal is allowed only for funded batches in which no payments have been posted against any of the invoices in the selected batch.

    A batch cannot be reversed if:

    • A funded batch cannot be reversed if a chargeback or recourse has been processed for any invoice in the batch.
    • A funded batch can be reversed only if no payments have been posted against any invoice in the selected batch.

  • Chargeback OverviewChargeback Overview

    The Chargeback hyperlink allows users to process a chargeback for a funded invoice.

    • Only one invoice can be chargebacked at a time.
    • After a chargeback is processed, the chargeback amount and any additional fees are deducted from the client’s Cash / Escrow Reserve.

      • Cash Reserve clients:
        Advance Balance + Accrued Fee

      • Escrow Reserve clients:
        Invoice Balance + Accrued Fee

    • Chargeback amount must be numeric and ≥ 0.01.

    • The entered amount cannot exceed the prefilled amount. Note that the Entered Amount cannot be greater than the Chargeback amount.

    • The chargeback amount cannot be empty

    Refer to the screenshot:

    Types of Chargebacks:

    • Complete Chargeback
      • A chargeback is treated as complete when the user does not modify the prefilled chargeback amount.
        • The invoice is removed from the Funded Invoices grid after processing.
        • If only fees remain (advance fully recovered), the invoice stays in the grid, and the prefilled chargeback amount equals the accrued fee balance.
    • Partial Chargeback
      • A chargeback is treated as partial when the user edits the prefilled amount.
        • The invoice remains in the Funded Invoices grid.
        • The invoice balance is reduced by the charged-back amount.

PO Number

 Displays the purchase order number for the invoice. If no PO is associated, this field is blank.

Invoice Date

 Displays the invoice date in MM/DD/YYYY format.

Due Date

Calculated based on the Invoice Date and the number of days in the selected payment term.

Example: Invoice Date: 01/05/2021, Payment Term: Net 30 → Due Date: 01/31/2021.

Invoice Amount

 Displays the original invoice amount.

Balance Amount

Displays the current outstanding balance of the invoice. The balance is reduced when a partial payment (PP) or partial chargeback (CB) is processed.

When the balance reaches 0, the invoice is removed from the Funded Invoices grid.

Follow-up Date

 Displays the follow-up date entered in the Comments pop-up at the invoice level. If not entered, the field is blank.

Estimated Payment Date

 Displays the estimated payment date entered in the Comments pop-up. If not entered, the field is blank.

Fund Date

 Displays the date on which the invoice batch was funded in MM/DD/YYYY format. This field is never blank.
Fee Name

Displays the fee name assigned to the invoice.

Fee names are configured under Menu → Client Maintenance → Factoring Fee Setup.

Fee Days

Read-only value calculated based on the setup in Factoring Basic Parameters.

Fee days can be calculated from either the Invoice Date or Fund Date, as configured for the client.

Advance Amount

 Displays the funded advance amount for the invoice. This is a read-only numeric field.
Reserve Amount

Displays the reserve amount held for the invoice.

Calculated as: Reserve Amount = Invoice Amount – Advance Amount
Earned Upfront

Displays the earned upfront fee amount with two decimal places.

Refer to Factoring Fee Setup for tier-based and per-day fee computation logic.
Accrued Fee

Displays the accrued fee balance for the invoice. This is a read-only numeric field with two decimal places.

The accrued fee value is never negative for any invoice in the Funded Invoices grid.

Accrued Fee Calculation

Total fees on an invoice consist of Earned Upfront Fee + Accrued Fee and are calculated based on the invoice’s Fee Days and the applicable Total Fee % from the client’s fee tier.

Formulas

  • Total Fee = Earned Upfront Fee + Accrued Fee
  • Earned Upfront Fee = (Invoice Amount or Advance Amount) × Total Fee %
  • Accrued Fee = Total Fee − Earned Upfront Fee
